Courses Available

Information Technology

This course introduces the fundamentals of Information Technology (IT) and covers essential topics including computer hardware, software applications, networking, cybersecurity, databases, and programming basics. Students will gain hands-on experience in configuring systems, managing networks, troubleshooting, and using common software tools to solve real-world problems. The course also explores the role of IT in business, emphasizing project management, ethical practices, and emerging technologies such as cloud computing, AI, and machine learning.

Criminology

This course provides an in-depth examination of criminology, focusing on the nature, causes, and consequences of crime within society. Students will explore various criminological theories, the structure and functioning of the criminal justice system, and the roles of law enforcement, courts, and corrections. Key topics include types of crime, social and environmental factors influencing criminal behavior, victimology, and policy development for crime prevention and rehabilitation.

Hotel Management

A Business Management course is designed to give students a comprehensive understanding of the principles, practices, and strategies involved in managing a business. This course covers various aspects of business, including planning, organizing, leading, and controlling, preparing students for roles in leadership, administration, and entrepreneurial ventures. It focuses on developing analytical, organizational, and decision-making skills essential for managing both people and processes effectively in a range of industries.

BS in Education Major (Math)

A major in Education with a focus on Mathematics combines teaching methods with in-depth mathematical studies, preparing students to become effective math educators. The program typically covers a variety of math courses, such as algebra, calculus, statistics, geometry, and sometimes more advanced topics like differential equations and linear algebra, to build a strong foundation in mathematical concepts. Alongside these, the major emphasizes educational theory, curriculum design, and student assessment strategies specific to math education.

BS in Education Major (English)

An Education major with a specialization in English is a comprehensive program that prepares students to become effective English language arts (ELA) teachers at the elementary, middle, or high school levels. This major is tailored for those with a passion for language, literature, and teaching. blending studies in English content with pedagogical theory and practice to develop educators capable of inspiring students' reading, writing, and critical thinking skills.
